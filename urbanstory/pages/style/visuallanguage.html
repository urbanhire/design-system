Visual Language

Visual language functions as a way for us to strengthen our brand. The more consistent we are with our visuals, the more users can engage and improve their awareness of our brand. This page shall be used as a guide and explanation of how to make our illustration for web-based purposes.

Symbolic Description

We believed in equal opportunity.

We seek for universality.

We're demonstrating integrity.

We trust on agile technology.

So, how to make the illustrations?

Relax, we're not dictators, we just set some boundaries to make the brand looks and feels the same throughout the time. Feel free to experiment with the illustration, let your imagination run free! You'll be fine as long as you follow these guidelines below!

Use Borders, only on Borders.

As we're projecting transparency, it's a good idea to put on framing on the background of the illustrations. You can use borders on the frame's borders or any place you see convinient. But remember the foreground illustrations are no-border zone!

Be Dynamicly Active.

Put some movement on your illustrations. My suggestions are: using hands gestures as they do something actively like writing or putting some sheet somewhere.

Monochrome.

As we have two sides of the company, the job seeker and the employer, we're all agreed on the monochrome color scheme for the employer purposes. You can use gradients too!

Focal Focus.

But, you're welcome to use some different colour for a certain focus. But keep in mind the majority of the colour shall always be: monochrome!
